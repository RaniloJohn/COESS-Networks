# What is a Network?

In its simplest form, a **computer network** is a group of two or more computers that are linked together. This linkage allows them to share resources (like files or printers) and communicate with each other.

### The Core Components
Every network consists of three fundamental parts:

1. **Nodes**: These are the devices connected to the network. Examples include your laptop, smartphone, or a network printer.
2. **Links**: The connection between nodes. This can be physical wires (like Ethernet or Fiber Optics) or wireless signals (like Wi-Fi or Bluetooth).
3. **Protocols**: The set of rules that govern how data is formatted and transmitted. Without protocol, the nodes wouldn't "speak the same language."

### Why do we need them?
Networks allow us to:
- **Share hardware** (like a single printer for an entire office)
- **Share data** (cloud storage, shared folders)
- **Communicate** (email, instant messaging, video calls)

> [!TIP]
> Think of a network like a conversation. The computers are the people, the Wi-Fi is the air they speak through, and the language they use (English, Spanish, etc.) is the protocol.
