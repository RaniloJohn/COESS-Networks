# IPv4 Subnetting Basics

Subnetting is the process of dividing a single large network into multiple smaller, manageable sub-networks (subnets). 

### Why Subnet?
- **Efficiency:** Prevents the waste of IP addresses.
- **Security:** Isolates traffic between departments.
- **Performance:** Reduces broadcast traffic.

### The Math: Binary & Masks
Every IP address is paired with a **Subnet Mask**. The mask tells the computer which part of the address belongs to the **Network** and which part belongs to the **Host**.

- **Network Bits:** Identifies the specific subnet.
- **Host Bits:** Identifies the specific device on that subnet.

### CCNA Practice Formula
To find the number of usable hosts, we use the formula:
**$2^h - 2$**
Where **h** is the number of host bits. We subtract 2 for the **Network Address** and the **Broadcast Address**.
